WildAni4D: Towards 4D Animal Mesh Reconstruction

WildAni4D recovers temporally coherent 3D animal geometry, articulated motion, and global trajectories from monocular in-the-wild videos by combining a scalable synthetic animal video pipeline with a video-native reconstruction model.

Abstract

Recovering animal geometry and motion over time from ordinary monocular videos.

Recovering 4D animal motion, including 3D geometry and global trajectory, is essential for quantitative biomechanics and behavioral analysis. However, progress is hindered by the lack of annotated animal video data and the temporal instability of per-frame reconstruction methods. WildAni4D addresses these challenges with a framework that unites a synthetic video generation pipeline and a new reconstruction model. Our generator creates realistic, appearance-consistent video sequences with ground-truth animal geometry and camera trajectories. Our Animal Video Transformer estimates temporally coherent motion using a single sequence-level shape and per-frame pose predictions. The resulting system drastically reduces temporal pose flicker and shape drift, enabling large-scale 4D animal reconstruction and downstream applications including motion annotation, animatable animal reconstruction, and text-to-motion generation.

Key Contributions

A complete data-and-model pipeline for video-native animal reconstruction.

WildAni4D-Gen

A scalable synthetic video generation pipeline that combines dynamic textured animals, diverse 3D scenes, and realistic camera motions to produce annotated animal videos.

Animal Video Transformer

A two-stage reconstruction model that uses camera trajectory estimation and temporal transformer features to recover world-grounded 4D animal motion from monocular video.

Sequence-level shape consistency reduces frame-wise drift and supports pseudo-ground-truth annotation, animation, and motion-generation applications.

Method Overview

WildAni4D first builds synthetic animal videos with full supervision, then learns temporally coherent reconstruction from video sequences.

WildAni4D-Gen. The generation pipeline synthesizes realistic animal videos by combining SMAL-based shape and motion, text-guided texture generation, diverse 3D scenes, and simulated camera trajectories. The rendered videos provide ground-truth SMAL parameters, 3D keypoints, masks, camera trajectories, and depth maps.

Animal Video Transformer. Temporal modeling is applied after the ViT backbone. The decoder predicts per-frame SMAL poses and translations while enforcing a single sequence-level shape parameter.

Why video-native?

Single-image animal reconstruction methods can produce plausible meshes per frame, but often flicker over time and drift in shape. WildAni4D explicitly separates identity from motion by predicting one shape for the full video sequence and per-frame articulated motion, which better matches the physical constraints of an animal moving through the world.
